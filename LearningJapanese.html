I/ Hiragana and Katakana

There is multiples alphabet in japanese, the :

  • Hiragana : The Hiragana's alphabet is a syllabary alphabet composed with 50 signs, it's used in the writing of japanese word like sushi or katana or origami when you don't know the kanji or in the conjugation.
  • Katakana : The Katakana's alphabet is a syllabary alphabet composed also with 50 signs, it's used in the writing of foreign words like beer wich is prononced biru or for the onomatopoeia.
  • Kanji : As the hiraganas, the kanjis are used to write the japanese words, there is one kanji for one word, the kanjis have two pronunciation, the On'yomi pronunciation and the Kun'yomi pronunciation, both have to be learned and can be used in differents context
  • Romaji : It's the latine alphabet used in english or french, it can be used for brand, foreign word or other... This alphabet is usually used.
  • Arabiasuji : Number's alphabet, there is kanjis and hiraganas for numbers but for mathematic, arabiasuji like 12345 are usually used

2. Simple form sentence

a. Affirmative sentence

I'm a student --> Watashi wa gakusee desu / わたし は がくせえ です

I'm french --> Watashi wa furansujin desu / わたし は ふらんすじん です

We can see that the wa after the watashi is writing "ha/は" instead of "wa/わ" which is an exception.

This "wa" which can be assimilated as the verb be is writing "ha/は" instead of "wa/わ"

You're an actor --> Anata wa haiyuu desu / あなた は はいゆう です

3. First Kanjis

Be sure to well know the hiragana and katakana before beginning the learning of the kanjis. There will be no more romaji in the tables, only hiragana or katakana (and kanji).

The kanjis might have 2 readings/pronunciation:

  • The On'yomi reading : Pronunciation which come from china, the sino-japanese reading
  • The Kun'yomi reading : The native reading, purely japanese

This first kanjis have only an Kun'yumi pronunciation.

This kanjis will be useful as themself and with other kanjis later.
